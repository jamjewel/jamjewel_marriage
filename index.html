Events

1st January

Engagement 6PM Onwards

This ceremony happens after the Aacharakalyanam is the Manasamantham ceremony, i.e. the betrothal. The bride’s family, as well as the groom’s family, get together at a church where the priest confirms that both families accept this wedding in presence of two witnesses. This ceremony concludes with a feast..

2nd January (Bride & Family)
3rd January (Groom & Family)

Madhuram Veppu 7PM Onwards

Madhuram Veppu is a Traditional Function among Kerala Christian Community Weddings. A night before the marriage, the sweet offering (to bride/groom) is conducted. The parents of the bride/groom and close relatives shall give sweets like cakes, chocolates, wine, etc. As per the Christian wedding tradition.

5th January

Wedding 5.30PM Onwards

This is the main event, The bride and groom along with their families reach the church on the wedding day Soon the priest performs the wedding ceremony by reading the Holy Bible. After that, the couple exchange wedding rings. After that, the groom ties a Mangalsutra around the neck of the bride after which they are declared Husband and Wife. This is followed by a grand feast.

Ugh... dress codes?

Mehndi 1PM - 5PM

Ethnic indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas along with an optional shawl.

Cocktail Night 7PM - 12AM

Western attire
A semi-formal look that is contemporary & fashionable which is not too prim & proper is the best to pull off this night. You can see some pins for ladies & gents.

Wedding 6PM-12AM

Indo-western
Ladies can flaunt their unconventional sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.
